# Learning Edges

A living document of areas where you're pushing your understanding. Updated by you and your runtime together during sessions. Used by the steward for spaced retrieval practice.

**How to use this file:**
- Add a new section when you notice yourself deferring something because you don't understand it (not just for efficiency)
- Be honest about your current level — the point is accuracy, not ego
- Let the runtime generate retrieval questions after teaching moments
- Review periodically: move things to "Pushed Through" when they're no longer edges
- The steward can pull questions from here for Signal check-ins

## Notes

- "Current level" should be specific. "I know some JavaScript" is useless. "I can write React components but don't understand the reconciliation algorithm or when useEffect cleanup fires" is useful.
- Retrieval questions should require thinking, not just recall. "What is X?" is weak. "You're in situation Y — what would you do and why?" is strong.
- It's fine to have 3-5 active edges. More than that and you're spreading too thin. Prioritize the ones that are blocking real work.
- Not everything needs to be a learning edge. Plenty of things are fine to permanently delegate. The test: would understanding this make you materially better at your job?
