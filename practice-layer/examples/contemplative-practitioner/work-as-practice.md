# Work as Practice

The headline stance. How I do the work matters as much as what I ship. This isn't productivity optimization; it's recognition that **the work itself is meditation material** — the pattern of restlessness, bargaining, avoidance, reaching, landing, releasing — all of it is visible on the desk in a way it isn't always visible on the cushion.

> *"The escape impulse is itself practice material."*

The reach for distraction when something is hard is not a failure to overcome. It's the exact thing to turn toward. The cushion teaches me to sit with aversion; the desk teaches me the same lesson in a different register, with more at stake.

---

## Three tiers I work in

Not a hierarchy of worth — a recognition of what each kind of work does for me and the world.

**Tier 3 — Distraction / avoidance.** Scrolling, tab-hopping, busywork disguised as research. Not evil, but eroding. When I notice I'm here, the move is not shame; it's curiosity — *what was I about to do that made this feel safer?*

**Tier 2 — Productive busywork.** Refactoring, infrastructure, cleaning data, rearranging systems. Real work. Often necessary. Sometimes a hiding place from Tier 1. The question is not "is this worth doing?" but "is this what I'm doing to avoid the thing that actually moves the week?"

**Tier 1 — Hard stuff that exposes me.** Cold outreach. Publishing something I'm not sure about. Making the call where the answer might be no. Shipping before it's polished. These are the things where the protective part of me — the self-image, in traditional framing — is at risk. And that risk is precisely why they move things.

A week of pure Tier-2 with zero Tier-1 is a drift pattern worth naming.

---

## How the fetters show up on the desk

Each fetter (see `wholesomeness-lens.md`) has a characteristic desk-form:

- **Bargaining** — "If I just research one more week, I'll be ready to send." The research becomes the ritual.
- **Conceit** — Over-preparing the message so no one can judge the draft. Or shrinking, apologizing in the subject line.
- **Restlessness** — Four tabs open, each one briefly visited, no decision made. The body can't sit with the discomfort of the one hard task.
- **Identity view** — "I'm not the kind of person who cold-emails." The self-image refuses to be tested.

Naming the form precisely is almost half the work. The other half is the tiny willingness to act from something other than the fetter.

---

## Not a grind

Work-as-practice does not mean work all the time. The opposite, often. When I'm in the work, I'm in it fully. When I'm not, I'm not. The **transition** is the skill — stepping away cleanly, returning cleanly.

Ajahn Chah: *"You think too much. That's why you suffer."* The desk makes this concrete. The rumination about the work while not doing the work is neither rest nor progress. Recognize it, sit for two breaths, choose one: return to the work, or actually rest.

---

## What I want from the model

- **Notice tier patterns over days, not moments.** A tier-2 day is not a failure. A tier-2 week with zero tier-1 is a drift signal.
- **Surface the fetter, not the task.** "What are you avoiding?" is less useful than "This feels like bargaining — are you waiting for permission that isn't coming?"
- **Don't manufacture urgency.** If there's no real deadline, don't pretend there is. Pressure that isn't real erodes trust in the ones that are.
- **Celebrate the return, not the streak.** Coming back after drift is the practice. Treat it as the win it is.
