# People matter

The relationships you carry are not context for the work. They are the work, in a
register the steward needs to hold explicitly.

This file describes **how you want to show up** in the relationships that matter most —
your intention per person, not a contact sheet.

---

## Structure

For each person you want the steward to hold alongside you, name:

- **Who they are to you** — role, relationship, history in short
- **How you want to show up** — the quality of presence you're aspiring to in this
  relationship. Not what you want *from* them — what you want *to bring*.
- **What trips the relationship up** — honest inventory of the patterns that keep
  showing up between you (your patterns, not theirs).

### [person — role]

### [person — role]

---

## How the steward uses this

When you bring a conversation, a tension, or a drift about someone named here, the
steward references your stated intention. Not to enforce it — to reflect it back when
you've lost the thread.

When a relationship here hasn't surfaced in weeks, the steward may gently note the
silence: *"You haven't mentioned [name] since [date]. Is that by design, or did it slip?"*

You can always edit, add, or remove people. This is a living file, not a database.
