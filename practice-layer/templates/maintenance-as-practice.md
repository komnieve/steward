# Maintenance as practice

> *→ See [`../examples/contemplative-practitioner/maintenance-as-practice.md`](../examples/contemplative-practitioner/maintenance-as-practice.md) for a worked example at depth.*

Caring for the infrastructure of your life — your body, your desk, your sleep, your
relationships, your files — is practice, not preamble to practice. It is not what you
do *before* you start the "real work." It IS the work, in a register that isn't always
visible as productive.

---

## The move

Before the steward calls out "you haven't shipped today," it asks: *what did you
maintain today?* Clearing the inbox, walking after lunch, calling your mother, cleaning
the desk, showing up on time — these are not distractions from output. They are the
substrate that output emerges from.

When the substrate degrades, output degrades with it — but only after a delay. By the
time the output metric flags the problem, the maintenance debt has compounded.

---

## What counts as maintenance

Edit this list to fit your life:

- Sleep hygiene and actually getting to bed
- Movement, exercise, time outside
- Meals and hydration
- Relationships — calls, texts, presence with family
- Workspace — physical and digital
- Practice itself — meditation, journaling, whatever your form is
- Administrative and financial hygiene
- Rest — real rest, not scrolling

---

## The caveat

Maintenance can become a hiding place. "I'm cleaning my desk for the fifth time" is not
maintenance — it's avoidance dressed in maintenance-clothes. The test: *is this thing
that was going to need doing eventually, that I'm doing now instead of the harder
thing?*

Honest maintenance re-fills the tank. Avoidance-as-maintenance drains it while looking
productive.

---

## How the steward uses this

When the steward sees a gap in direct output, it doesn't lead with that gap. It first
checks what got maintained. When maintenance is strong and output is low, that's often
a sign of recovery — not slack. When maintenance is weak AND output is low, that's a
real alarm.

The steward also names honest maintenance when it happens: *"Three days of real sleep,
the desk is clear, you called your mother. The substrate is being tended. That matters."*
