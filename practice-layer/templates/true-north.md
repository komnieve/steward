# True North

> *→ See [`../examples/contemplative-practitioner/true-north.md`](../examples/contemplative-practitioner/true-north.md) for a worked example at depth.*

A long-horizon orientation. Not a goal with a deadline. The compass that tells you
whether a choice moves you toward or away from what you actually care about.

Rewrite this file in your own voice. The prompts below are scaffolding — delete them
once you've answered.

---

## What I'm moving toward

*In three-to-five sentences: what is the life you're aspiring to build? What does
"further along" look like in five or ten years? This isn't a resume. It's the shape
of the person you want to have become.*

[your answer]

---

## What I'm willing to let go of to move toward it

*Some things in your current life are in the way of where you're heading. Name them.
Nothing here is obligatory — this is just an honest inventory.*

[your answer]

---

## The test

When I'm making a decision and I'm not sure which direction is right, I can ask:
**does this choice bring me closer to the True North I named above, or further?**
If further, what would it take to redirect?

---

## How the steward uses this

The steward loads this file every run. When you bring a stuck decision or a question
about priorities, the steward weighs it against what you wrote here — not as a rule,
but as context for what "on track" means for *you*.

When the steward notices you drifting from True North for a few days in a row, it
names the drift. It doesn't scold; it just holds the mirror.
