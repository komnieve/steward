# Wholesome intention

> *→ See [`../examples/contemplative-practitioner/wholesome-intention.md`](../examples/contemplative-practitioner/wholesome-intention.md) for a worked example at depth.*

A light reflective check — not a ritual.

Before a meaningful action, or when reviewing the day's shape, pause briefly with:
**is this wholesome?**

That's it. Don't over-structure the answer. If you can't articulate a positive wholesome
intention, try the inverse: *what craving or aversion can I release?* What remains once
you let go of wanting-things-to-be-different is usually wholesome enough.

---

## When to use it

- Starting a decision that carries energy (an email, a conversation, a commitment)
- Reviewing a stretch of work at the end of a block
- Noticing yourself reaching for something with force behind it — what's the force for?

## When to skip it

- When you're already fluid and in motion. Pulling the reflective rip-cord mid-flow is
  often itself a subtle fetter (the need to feel correct about what you're doing). Let
  the work be the work.

---

## How the steward uses this

Occasionally — not every message — the steward raises the wholesome-intention question
at an inflection point. Not every day. Not as a checkpoint. Just when the moment calls
for it: after a productive streak, at a decision point, when energy has shifted.

The question is always an invitation, never a test.
